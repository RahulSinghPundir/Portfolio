Mental Health Analysis


In this project, I developed a system using Natural Language Processing (NLP) techniques and machine learning algorithms to analyze comments related to mental health. The goal was to predict whether a comment is poisonous or not, aiming to identify harmful or toxic content that could impact individuals' mental well-being in online communities or social media platforms. I collected a dataset of mental health-related comments, preprocessed the data using NLP techniques, and built classification models using machine learning algorithms such as SVM, Naive Bayes, or Random Forests. The models were trained on labeled data and evaluated using cross-validation and metrics like accuracy, precision, recall, and F1-score. The system's potential lies in enhancing online safety and mental health support by enabling platforms to take prompt action against toxic comments. Through this project, I gained practical experience in NLP, machine learning, data preprocessing, and evaluation, while also deepening my understanding of the importance of mental health support in the digital space.
